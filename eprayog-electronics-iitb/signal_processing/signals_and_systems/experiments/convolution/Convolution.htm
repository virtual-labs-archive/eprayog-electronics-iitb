EXPERIMENT 1: DISCRETE TIME CONVOLUTION

 

Objective: To understand the concept of convolution and simulate simple programs related to convolution in Scilab and implement them on TMS320C6713, TMS320VC5510 and TMS320VC33 kits.

Pre Session Work: Convolution is a mathematical way of combining two signals to form a third signal. It has applications that include statistics, computer vision, image and signal processing, electrical engineering, and differential equations.

For complex-valued functions ƒ, g defined on the set Z of integers, the discrete convolution of ƒ and g is given by:

 

Consider two signals f[n] and g[n]. Convolution of these signals involves the following steps

1.      Folding: Fold g[m] about m=0 and obtain g[-m].

2.      Shifting: Shift g[-m] by n to the right (left) if n is positive (negative), to obtain g[n - m].

3.      Multiplication: Multiply f[m] by g[n - m] to obtain the product sequence f[m]g[n - m].

4.      Summation: Sum of all values of the product sequence to obtain the value of output.
